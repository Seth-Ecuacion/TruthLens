Title: DeepFakes and Beyond: A Survey of Face Manipulation and Fake Detection | Source: Tolosana, R., Vera-Rodriguez, R., Fierrez, J., Morales, A., & Ortega-Garcia, J. | Date: 2020

This technical survey provides a comprehensive review of AI-driven facial manipulation techniques and the methods developed to detect them, organized around four main manipulation categories driven by the proliferation of Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs) and the accessibility of large-scale public datasets.

The four manipulation types are entire face synthesis (generating non-existent faces from scratch using architectures like StyleGAN and ProGAN), identity swap (replacing one person's face with another in video, what is commonly called "DeepFakes"), attribute manipulation (modifying specific facial features such as age, gender, or hair color, as seen in apps like FaceApp), and expression swap (transferring facial expressions from one person to another in video, using techniques like Face2Face and NeuralTextures). Identity swap databases have evolved across two generations, with first-generation fakes showing visible artifacts and low quality, while second-generation databases like Celeb-DF and the Deepfake Detection Challenge (DFDC) dataset produce significantly more realistic content that is harder to detect.

Detection methods range from analyzing GAN-specific fingerprints and steganalysis features to deep learning approaches using CNNs, attention mechanisms, and temporal modeling via RNNs. While detection systems perform well under controlled conditions, often achieving near-perfect accuracy, they consistently fail to generalize to unseen databases and manipulation techniques, with most approaches dropping below 60% AUC against second-generation deepfake databases. Social network compression and resizing further degrade detection performance. The survey identifies GAN fingerprint removal as an emerging threat that can fool even advanced detectors, and calls for more diverse public databases, standardized benchmarks, and detection methods that do not require fake video samples for training to improve generalization.
